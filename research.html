    PhD Thesis

  • Gupta, A. (2022). BERT for Discourse Analysis: A Pragmatist Approach to Governmentality. University of Bristol (Awarded with minor corrections 21/3/2023).
    Code

    This thesis explores the utility of deep learning language models as a tool for understanding how language is used in politics. Using a dataset of 92 million tokens constructed from British legislation and central departments, I used word sense induction via BERT to analyse the British state's vocabulary and the diffusion of discourses about 'resilience', 'sustainability' and 'wellbeing' across the organisations of the British state during 2000 - 2020. I developed a theoretical framework drawing on perspectives from pragmatics, Foucauldian work on governmentality and sociolinguistics to interpret the results of the deep learning assisted data analysis.
